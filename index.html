Mobile Trauma Units come to Claremont Village

By Jason Gonzalez

New York City Council Member Vanessa Gibson continues to wag war on crime and violence in the Bronx. Less than a year ago, Gibson spearheaded the ‘mobile trauma unit’, and within a short period of time, mobile trauma units are becoming a staple in the Bronx communities that matter most. Gibson alongside Bronx District Attorney Darcel Clark as well as members of the organization BronxConnect showcased two mobile trauma units serving the section of Claremont Village.

The mobile trauma units are all one level vans, staffed with social workers and trauma unit therapists as well. Mobile unit personnel intends to provide the families of homicide victims, as well as other sufferers of violent crimes with the proper emotional and psychological support needed.

District 16 Councilwoman Vanessa Gibson and Bronx District Attorney Darcel Clark pose for a photo in front of the parked mobile trauma unit.

Not to be confused, all mobile trauma units will provide services to members from other jurisdictions. Individuals from different communities also have the option of traveling to different mobile units depending on the severity of the emergency. The mobile trauma unit can and will travel to high need areas. Discretion will be utilized and considered on a case-by-case basis. “It depends where the unit is. It’s always going to be open to whoever comes up to them,” Bronx D.A. Clark said. “So, they are not going to turn somebody away. ‘Where do you live? Oh, I am sorry we are not in your...’So that’s not going to happen.”

This concerted effort to help communities ravaged by grief is a resource that many elected officials hope is utilized. This should be taken advantage of.

Based on the findings on the map, more mobile trauma units should be housed in the communities of Concourse Village, Mott Haven and Crotona Park.
